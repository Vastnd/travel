Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji, or Fuji-san, is the highest mountain in Japan, standing at 12,388 feet (3,776 meters). Located on Honshu Island near the Pacific coast, it is a dormant volcano that last erupted in 1707. Mount Fuji is a sacred symbol of Japan and a popular destination for climbers and tourists

Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza, built around 2600 BC, is the largest of the Egyptian pyramids and served as the tomb for Pharaoh Khufu. It is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World and the only one that remains largely intact. Originally standing at 481 feet (146.5 meters), it was the tallest man-made structure in the world for over 3,800 years

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a stunning white-marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Construction began around 1632 and took about 22 years to complete. It is renowned for its architectural beauty, blending Indian, Persian, and Islamic styles, and is a UNESCO World Heritage site
